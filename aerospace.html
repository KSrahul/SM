Our Aerospace and Defense Industry

India’s aerospace and defense electronics market is expected to be worth $70-72 billion over the next 10 to 12 years as the country rapidly modernizes its military by embracing new technologies [1]. Strong infrastructure push from government has put the Indian civil aviation market on a solid profitable growth path and is projected to become the third largest by 2020 from its current ninth position [1].

The ‘Make in India’ government campaign seeks to cut the dependence on imported weapons and positions the country as a hub for defense manufacturing, this requires the Indian A&D manufacturers to develop cutting edge platforms and systems to not only keep up with the high demands but also adhere to strict compliance standards and maintain cost controls at the same time.

While the sector growth is largely promising, the sector is still crippled with challenges like legacy technology, limited funding, and lack of easy access to the raw materials. To effectively compete in the global marketplace the sector needs to utilize full strength of digitalization capabilities and make efficient use of large pool of available manpower. The new entrants need to quickly adapt modern tools and technologies that can help them drive innovation, reduce design and manufacturing times and costs and allow manufacturing of high-precision parts with pervasive security and reliability.
